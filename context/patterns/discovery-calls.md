# Discovery Call Process

How Pulse runs discovery calls to understand a client's business problem.

## Approach

Discovery is about understanding the business process at a functional level — not jumping to solutions.

## Call Structure

### 1. Find the Trigger Point
- What event or action kicks off the process?
- Who initiates it? What prompts them?

### 2. Walk the Current Process
- Step by step: what happens after the trigger?
- Who's involved at each step?
- What tools/systems are used?
- Where does information flow between people or systems?

### 3. Map Edge Cases
- What happens when things go wrong?
- What are the exceptions to the normal flow?
- How are these handled today? (Manual workarounds, escalation, etc.)

### 4. Document as Flow Charts
- Translate the conversation into user flow charts
- Show the process visually — trigger → steps → decision points → outcomes
- Present back to the client to confirm: "this is what we heard — is this right?"

## Output

- Process flow charts that demonstrate understanding of the client's problem
- Confirmation from the client that Pulse has captured the process accurately
- Foundation for building the project scope and user stories

## Key Principle

The goal is to show the client that you understand their problem before proposing any solution. Discovery builds trust and prevents building the wrong thing.
